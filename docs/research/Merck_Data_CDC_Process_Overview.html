Continuous Manufacturing Process Overview

  • This specific data set consists of 10 worksheets of data collected from a 120 hour study in which Machine Level & Contextual Quality data was collected to asses the effects of raw material changes during the study.
  • Original worksheets: LiW Feeders 1, LiW Feeders 2, Blenders, Tablet Press, Humidity, Temperature, RM Tablet Properties and Drum Change, RM Content Uniformity, RM Material Properties, Logbook Long Run Days
  • The LiW feeders are used to continuously feed individual raw materials into the inlet of the first blender.
  • Here the now in-process materials, which we will now refer too as powder, flows through the first blender which is (ideally) optimizied based on the setup of the mixing blades & product type configurations (Recipe, Volume, etc) to control the amount of shear imparted on the powder which is imperative to ensure the mixing is sufficient enough.
  • Upon exit of Blender 1, which is directly coupled to the inlet of a Blender 2 along with a LiW Feeder for the lubricant.
  • Blender 2 is responsible for the blending of any components of the formulation which are shear sensitive such as in this case is the lubricant.
  • Having two blending stages allows for maximum flexibility within a process as it allows for these two blending stages to be decoupled.
  • Blender 2 was configured to minimize shear and in process material Mean Residence Time (MRT). In the case of lubrication blending this minimizes the risk of over lubrication of the blend, which can lead to issues with the final product.

Machines/Equipment

ConsiGma 116 TM CDC-50 (GEA Group, Wommelgem, Belgium)

The GEA CDC-50 Rig was constructed by integrating three units into a single processing platform. This was done by combining the GEA LiW Feeding System with ConsiGma Dosing and Blending (DB) Modules as well as the Nexgen Press Range.

GEA LIW Loss in Weight Feeder

The LiW Feeder is able to measure the loss in weight through a discharge device that withdraws material from the hopper and meters it to a downstream process. The weighing mechanism or scale weighs the all material in the Hopper, The Hopper Itself, and The Discharge Device itself but, Doesn’t Weigh Components in The Inactive Section. When the weighing mechanism’s weight reading changes, the controller can direct the motor speed controller on the discharge device to deliver more or less material to the process. When the material level in the hopper reaches or falls below a specified threshold, the controller signals for a refilling agent (Automatically or Human) to refill the hopper. The controller ensures that material continues to feed the process while the hopper is refilling. As the discharge device moves material from the hopper into the downstream process, the weight of material in the hopper decreases. The controller evaluates the amount of LiW sensed by the weighing mechanism over a given time interval to determine the Feedrate. The controller adjusts the discharge device’s speed via the motor speed controller to either increase or decrease the loss in weight which is the Feedrate.

Nexgen Press Range

NexGen Press Process Steps:

1. Fill: Powder fills a die cavity.

2. Pre-Compress: Upper and lower punches apply pressure, influenced by the Air Compensator, for uniform filling.

3. Main Compression: Punches apply significant force, again regulated by the Air Compensator, forming the tablet.

4. Eject: The finished tablet is ejected, ready for downstream processing.

Raw Material Feeding (Loss-in-Weight Feeders)

Raw materials are continuously introduced into the manufacturing line using loss-in-weight (LiW) feeders, which gravimetrically control material flow by measuring weight loss over time. Each feeder maintains a target mass flow rate, ensuring accurate material ratios and steady-state operation.

Five LiW feeders (PD1–PD5) deliver individual powder components into the first continuous blender. Feeder control systems dynamically adjust operating parameters, such as screw speed, to correct deviations from desired flow rates and maintain consistent feeding.

Initial Blending Stage (Blender 1)

The material streams from PD1–PD5 enter Blender 1, where powders are combined and mixed under continuous operation. This blender provides sufficient residence time and shear to produce a homogeneous intermediate blend.

Under normal operating conditions, the outlet mass flow rate of Blender 1 matches the total inflow from the upstream feeders, indicating steady-state behavior.

Lubricant Addition and Final Blending Stage (Blender 2)

The output of Blender 1 feeds directly into Blender 2, where the final blend is prepared. At this stage, a separate LiW feeder (PD7) introduces the lubricant into the powder stream.

Blender 2 ensures uniform distribution of the lubricant throughout the blend while minimizing over-lubrication. In steady operation, the mass flow exiting Blender 2 equals the sum of the Blender 1 outflow and the lubricant feeder inflow.

Nexgen Press Range (Tablet Press)

The final powder blend exits Blender 2 and is transferred to a rotary tablet press, where it is compacted into tablets. Tablet formation occurs in two main stages:

  • Pre-compression, during which the powder is lightly compacted as the bottom punch moves upward while the upper punch remains fixed.
  • Main compression, where higher compression forces are applied to form tablets with the desired weight, thickness, and mechanical strength.

Tablet press control systems adjust parameters such as compression force and die fill depth to ensure consistent tablet quality.

Tablet Routing and Quality Control

After compression, tablets are routed based on real-time quality assessment:

  • Accepted tablets that meet predefined quality specifications proceed to the coating unit.
  • Rejected tablets that fall outside acceptable limits are diverted from the process.
  • Sampled tablets are periodically removed for offline quality testing, including weight, thickness, and hardness measurements.

Tablet Coating

Accepted core tablets are transferred to a continuous tablet coater, where a thin film coating is applied. This step improves tablet stability, appearance, and functionality while maintaining continuous material flow through the manufacturing line.

Process Monitoring and Control Strategy

LiW feeders employ local feedback control to maintain accurate mass flow rates. Deviations from target conditions trigger automatic parameter adjustments to restore steady operation.

The tablet press utilizes local feedback control to regulate compression force and tablet weight. Tablets produced during periods of out-of-spec operation are automatically rejected.

A residence time distribution (RTD) framework enables traceability of material through the system. When process disturbances or alarms occur, material produced during the affected time window is identified and classified as out-of-spec, ensuring robust quality assurance in continuous manufacturing.
